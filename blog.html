Why We Have Our Focus

Posted by Nancy Pickett, July 31, 2015

When your child has cancer, everyone in the hospital becomes family. Security guards, janitors, and ER nurses recognize you just because of how frequently you visit the hospital. The hematology/oncology floor is a tight knit community, but they're not the only ones who know the hospital inside out. There are thousands of children living with life altering or life threatening chronic conditions, with little to no known treatments. Just like pediatric cancer patients, these kids miss out on their childhood and can die entirely too early due to dangerous treatments and lack of funding. Some chronic illnesses are treated with chemo, and some are more deadly than many types of cancer. Chronic illness needs awareness too.. So why is The Golden Truth focused entirely on pediatric oncology?

Statistic

Posted by Nancy Pickett, June 22, 2015

2015 has been filled with bad news. As of today at least 43,250 children have died from cancer, and to me it feels like more. I can't scroll through any form of social media without reading about a child's funeral, hospice care plan, or relapse. At first I thought this overwhelming amount of bad news was me getting more immersed in this community. But it's not. Pediatric cancer incidence rates are climbing, and so are the number of deaths. The treatments we're using to kill childhood cancer are giving kids more cancer. (Yes, you did read that correctly. Cancer treatments are causing secondary cancer.) And if it's not another cancer, it's heart disease, kidney failure, immune difficiancies, the list goes on. The other day, I had a kid tell me that they felt better before their tumor was found, because the pain from that was less than the pain caused by chemo. That's absolutely unacceptable, and I could list off a million statistics about why! But I'm not going to do that, because these kids are not statistics. They're kids.
