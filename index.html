Welcome to The Golden Truth

The Golden Truth is a documentary film that will change the way the world sees pediatric oncology. Childhood cancer has been mispotrayed by the media for years. The pictures of smiling bald children that we see online make it easy for people to forget the battles that these kids are facing. Often, people don't realize that the medicines being pumped into children are dangerous chemicals at levels intended for adults. People don't see kids vomiting until they're too tired to move. People don't realize that children fighting cancer are fighting for their lives. We believe that it is important to show the beeping machines and the sad faces, not because we want to see it- but because we want to change it. After all, you can’t fix a problem unless you know it’s there. Our film will uncover the shocking truth about pediatric cancer, and offer hope to those in the fight. (read more)

Meet the Kids

Kylie Myers

After working for a month to find out what was wrong with Kylie's left leg, Kylie was diagnosed with Ewing's Sarcoma on April 9th, 2014. After her family cried and prayed together, she said, "God must have a great, big plan for me." Although we wish the plan had included healing, Kylie sadly passed away on February 13, 2015. That has not stopped her life from making a difference. During treatment, she started "Smiley for Kylie", a place for her friends, family, and strangers to share smiles and keep her positive through the hard times - which she always was. Her positivity is something we should all strive to have.Before she passed, she gave her family a charge to kill childhood cancer so no other child would have to endure what she went through. They gladly take up the fight by raising awareness for childhood cancer and rallying for more research and safer treatments.

Talia Joy Castellano

After being diagnosed with neuroblastoma at 7 years old, Talia Joy discovered the magic of makeup. She took the attention away from her bald head, and focused it on her beautiful face. She began making makeup tutorials on YouTube, catching the attention of millions. Talia was featured on Ellen and was an honorary Covergirl. After battling childhood cancer for 6 years, Talia passed away on July 16th 2013. She was 13 years old. Her family keeps her legacy alive by raising money for research, and doing Covergirl makeovers for girls on treatment. For more information visit Talia's social media sites or taliaslegacy.org


Esther Earl

Esther Earl was a spunky, creative teenager who lost her life to thyroid cancer in 2010. A dedicated Nerdfighter, she was the inspiration for John Green's The Fault in Our Stars. Her diary has also been published as "This Star Won't Go Out" pictured to the right. Her family has also started TSWGO foundation , supporting families in financial distress because of childhood cancer treatments and expenses.


Ellie Walton

Ellie Walton is a brave 1 year old battling stage 3 brain cancer & hydrocephalus. She has had 7 surgeries, and is on her 7th round of chemotherapy. Ellie is beating all the odds, and fighting hard even when she is allergic to chemo! To support Ellie and her absolutely wonderful family, visit their Facebook: Prayers For Ellie Walton or instagram: @prayersforellie


Annabella Munoz

Annabella was diagnosed with Stage 3 Neuroblastoma on February 1st, 2013 at only 11 months old. This changed to stage 4 after more tumors were found in her legs. She had 8 rounds of chemo and tumor resection before she was announced "cancer free" on August 15, 2013. She will continue to scan every 3 months and will need to test for possible chemo side effects. "Team Bella" continues to spread awareness and raise money for childhood cancer. You can keep up with them on Facebook: Bella's Battle or instagram: @bellasbattlexoxo

Javen Nesbitt

Javen Nesbitt is not only a Sickle Cell patient, he is an actor, singer, gymnast, and all around good performer. Nothing can keep this kid down, he is extremely involved in community theatre performances and other productions despite hospital stays and treatments. We are excited to see him on Disney Channel one day!

Emma McDonald

Emma is a vibrant dancer and high school senior who was diagnosed with osteosarcoma in her leg. Despite relapse and multiple surgeries, she has kept up her grades, competed with her incredible dance team, and has been accepted into college! Emma aspires to be a Pediatric Oncology Nurse Practitioner to help kids who are going through the same journey she's been on.

Logan Finney

At just 4 years old, Logan Grace Finney was diagnosed with Pre B Cell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia. When she first started showing symptoms, everyone said that it was a virus. He mother refused to give up, demanding testing that finally showed her dangerous disease. After years of treatment far away from her home, she celebrated her end of treatment in October 2015.

Reef Carneson

Reef is an ALL Leukemia survivor currently on treatment for GVHD and other complications from chemotherapy. 2 years ago, Reef was confined to a stroller, could not talk, and was fed through a NG tube. Through the treatments received in the US, Reef is walking, talking, and eating on his own. Reef has defied the odds, and survived even when doctors were ready to give up. To donate so that Reef can finish his treatments, and for updates on his health, visit savebabyreef.com

Sara Willis

Sara lost her vision Monday October 21. She has a germinoma compressing her optic nerve. It is inoperable and malignant, but treatable. Her treatment so far has included a spinal tap, multiple MRIs, a craniotomy, and insertion of a port. She continues to fight childhood cancer, go to school, and raise awareness at events like CureFest!


Abby Snider

On March 1st, 2014 Abby was diagnosed with Tcell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia. Her total treatment will consist of 2 1/2 years of treatment at UVA Children's Hospital. Recently she has struggled with Pancreas problems, but nothing has kept Abby from raising awareness and enjoying her teenage years! Abby's faith has played a huge role in her journey, and she believes that God is the reason that she is still here and fighting.

Mikey Sclafani

Mikey passed away from neuroblastoma at 9 years old. He loved hockey and is described as strong, persevering, and compassionate. His sister is carrying out his legacy by raising awareness and advocating for childhood cancer. His family presents an award to students exhibiting Mikey's admirable qualities to students from his elementary school each year.

Desi Allman

Desi was diagnosed with ALL leukemia when she was just 5 years old. Her family was shocked to find out that what they thought was a soccer injury was actually cancer. Thankfully, she just finished chemo and is enjoying life away from the hospital.

Celena Butler

Celena Butler is a 3 year old ALL leukemia fighter. When she was first diagnosed, doctors didn’t think she would make it through the night- but she beat all the odds and entering her last year of treatment! She just finished enjoying her Make A Wish trip, and is looking forward to finishing treatment.



Kyarra Casado

Kyarra, or Yaya, has battled AML leukemia for years. She celebrated her end of treatment in August of 2014 after fighting for more than half of her life! Yaya loves anime, the beach, and ninja turtles and looks forward to a life outside the hospital.


Andrew McDonough

Andrew was a 14 year old athlete playing soccer when he went into cardiac arrest. In the hospital, he was diagnosed with Leukemia. The ICU said he was "the sickest kid they'd ever seen" and he suffered multiple hemorrhages, 4 strokes, and a major brain aneurysm that forced him to be airlifted for surgery. After a brave battle, Andrew passed away with his family by his side. His dad has now started the B+ foundation to help other families. It's named after Andrew's blood type and the way he lived his life. Find out more at bepositive.org

Samantha Mays

Samantha is a long term childhood cancer survivor. She continues to help children still fighting through friendship, prayer, and awareness. We met her at CureFest where she was speaking out and raising awareness for childhood cancers. Samantha loves makeup and is enjoying her cancer free life!


Courtney Ott

Courtney Ott is an osteosarcoma survivor dedicated to raising awareness and helping other children fighting cancer through friendship and prayer. She advocates for childhood cancer online and by speaking at schools, CureFest, and of course The Golden Truth!
