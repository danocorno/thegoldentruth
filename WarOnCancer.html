#WarOnCancer

Posted by Nancy Pickett, November 11, 2015

Is Starbucks waging a war on Christmas?! All over the news this week, we have heard about #WarOnChristmas. Thousands of people are blowing up their social media - arguing about, yes, a red paper cup. It's not even December, and we're already freaking out about the ornaments missing on our Peppermint Mochas. The #WarOnChristmas is getting way out of hand.

What bothers me the most about this social media phenomenon is how much people care about it. While millions of us sit inside our warm houses, drinking our $5 lattes, photoshopping seasonal cups into the hands of our favorite celebrities- we forget about the things that are actually important. Human trafficking is generating BILLIONS of dollars a year. Thousands of people do not have access to clean water. Women all across the developing world are denied an education. Thousands of children are dying of cancer. We don't see too much of that on Instagram.

We have a huge "exposure" problem in the more developed countries of the world. In order to hear about what really matters we have to seek it out - and let's face it: with a Twitter feed full of things as intriguing as #TheDress and Little Einsteins remixes - why would you do that?! I wonder how much of a better place the world would be if we were simply exposed to things that matter. Would we continue to turn our heads because the outside world is "too sad" to look at? Some of us would. But some of us would care, and work together to bring change - if only we knew what was going on.

The Golden Truth focuses on pediatric oncology. That's it. But in the future I hope to make a series of documentaries, exposing "truths" that we don't see every day. Awareness doesn't solve problems, but you can't solve a problem unless you know it is there. As you scroll through social media today, really think about what you're doing. Are you filling your mind with internet memes and celebrity gossip, completely oblivious to the suffering world around you? Or are you working to bring change to a world that desperately needs a shift in priorities?

Help #supportTRUTH, and support real news, real exposure, and the real world. Post pictures of your #WarOnCancer to promote positive change on social media.
